# About Toluwalemi Oluwadare

Toluwalemi Oluwadare — often called "Tolu" — is a Software Engineer based in Nigeria with over five years of experience building scalable backend systems for startups across healthtech, fintech, and agritech. His primary language is Python, and his go-to stack is Python, Django, and AWS.

What sets Toluwalemi apart is that he bridges two worlds that rarely meet: deep backend engineering experience and a business education. He is currently pursuing an MBA in Entrepreneurship and Operations Management at the University of Ibadan, which means he thinks about systems not just in terms of performance and correctness, but in terms of business impact, operational efficiency, and organisational strategy.

He has spent the bulk of his career inside fast-moving startups where he has worn multiple hats — from hands-on backend engineer to technical lead — and has developed a strong sense of what it means to ship software that actually moves a business forward.

## Personality and working style

Toluwalemi is the kind of engineer who cares about the "why" as much as the "how". He asks questions about business context before writing code, and he is deliberate about making engineering decisions that align with product and commercial goals. He is comfortable leading teams, running code reviews, and setting standards, but equally comfortable working as an individual contributor.

He describes himself as someone who "enjoys solving real business problems with intelligent systems." He is intellectually curious and is currently going deeper into AI Engineering — building RAG pipelines, working with LLMs, embeddings, and vector databases — because he believes AI is the next major surface where good engineers can create outsized value.

He values clear communication, async-friendly workflows, and a culture of ownership. He has worked with distributed teams across Nigeria and the United States and is fully comfortable with remote-first environments.

## Outside work

Toluwalemi has a famously eclectic range of interests. He likes to build backend systems — that part is obvious — but he also learns random things on the internet purely out of curiosity. Video editing today, Klingon tomorrow, plumbing some other day. He is the kind of person who will go deep on something completely unrelated to his job just because it caught his attention.

In his free time he reads fiction (lazily, by his own admission), plays eFootball and Fortnite, binge-watches TV shows, and types at 75 WPM — fast enough to keep up with his own thoughts, which is useful when you spend as much time in a terminal as he does. He keeps a public reading list at https://toluwalemi.com/reading/ for anyone who wants to see what he is currently reading.

This intellectual restlessness is not separate from his engineering — it is part of what makes him good at it. He learns quickly, picks up new tools and domains without much friction, and brings an unusual breadth of perspective to problem-solving.

## Entrepreneurial side

Beyond his engineering roles, Toluwalemi is the founder of Lemitek (lemitek.com), a company building digital products focused on business growth. One of Lemitek's flagship projects is PyWhisperer (pywhisperer.dev) — an AI-powered learning platform that teaches Python and AI technologies through personalised, interest-driven lessons. The idea is that learning should feel personal: whether you're into football, films, or cooking, PyWhisperer makes the technical content feel relevant to you.
